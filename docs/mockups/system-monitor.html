Creatrix · system-monitor-v1 · design mock · 2026-07-04

The frame budget stops being a rumor. Every effect instance shows its own cost, live.

Three surfaces from system-monitor-v1: the always-on statusbar meter cluster (PK.3, decision 26 — Ableton's CPU box, verbatim), the Activity-Monitor-style panel that breaks the frame budget down per effect instance — not per effect type (PK.1's anti-collapse fix) — with an actionable freeze button per row (PK.4→PK.5), and the same panel re-hosted as a detached OS window for multiwindow-stage-a (PK.6), polling at 2 Hz, menu-only, no hotkey anywhere per the T1 verdict. Numbered pins tie every affordance back to its packet.

1 — Statusbar clusterPK.3 · decision 26

Always visible, even at idle — the RAM meter no longer disappears at pressure level 'ok' the way today's MemoryStatus.tsx does. Percentage is rendered on top of the fill itself, the Ableton Live CPU-box pattern, not beside it. Mocked in the top transport bar's right cluster per this brief's wording — see Open Question 1 on the exact mount point.
